# The Killer 5
### 5 Things Silently Destroying Your Business Online

Use this checklist in every episode. Pull up the business on your phone and run through all 5 on camera.

---

## 1. The Google Ghost
**What it is:** You google their business name and either nothing shows up, the info is wrong, or their Google Business profile is a wasteland - no photos, no hours, no reviews responded to.

**Why it kills:** 90% of people find local businesses through Google on their phone. If you don't exist there, you don't exist.

**What to look for on camera:**
- Google their name - what shows up?
- Is the address/phone/hours correct?
- How many reviews? Are any responded to?
- Do they have photos or is it just the default Google street view?

---

## 2. The Mobile Disaster
**What it is:** Their site looks broken, unreadable, or unusable on a phone. Tiny text, sideways scrolling, buttons you can't tap, images covering everything.

**Why it kills:** 75%+ of their customers are finding them on a phone. If the site doesn't work on mobile, they just bounce to the next place.

**What to look for on camera:**
- Pull up their site on your phone
- Can you read anything without zooming?
- Do the buttons work? Can you find hours/location in 5 seconds?
- Does it load fast or does it hang?

---

## 3. The Time Capsule
**What it is:** The site looks like it was built in 2009 and never touched again. Outdated design, dead links, old menus, "Coming Soon" sections that never came, copyright 2017 in the footer.

**Why it kills:** An outdated site tells customers the business doesn't care about details. If your website is neglected, what else is?

**What to look for on camera:**
- What year does this site feel like?
- Check the copyright date in the footer
- Are there any dead links or broken images?
- Is the menu/pricing current?

---

## 4. The Identity Crisis
**What it is:** The vibe of the website doesn't match the vibe of the actual business. A beautiful upscale cocktail bar with a site that looks like a pizza delivery page. A trendy barbershop with a generic Wix template.

**Why it kills:** People decide if they're coming before they walk in. The website IS the first impression. If it doesn't match reality, you're either scaring people away or attracting the wrong crowd.

**What to look for on camera:**
- Film the actual business - show how it looks and feels in person
- Then show the website - does it match AT ALL?
- Would you go here based solely on the website?

---

## 5. The Dead End
**What it is:** There's no clear action for the customer to take. No online booking, no clickable phone number, no directions link, no menu, no "reserve now" button. The site is a digital brochure that leads nowhere.

**Why it kills:** Every person who visits the site and can't immediately book, call, or get directions is a lost customer. They won't work to find you - they'll just go somewhere easier.

**What to look for on camera:**
- Can you book / reserve / order from the site?
- Is the phone number clickable on mobile?
- Can you get directions in one tap?
- Is there ANY call to action or is it just... there?
